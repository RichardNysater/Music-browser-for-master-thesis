Below is a reference list of the perceptual features used.

  • Activity (low-high)
    • The activity of the music.
  • Articulation (legato-staccato)
    • The duration of tones, measured from legato (longest) to staccato (shortest).
  • Dissonance (consonant-dissonant)
    • The dissonance of the music.
  • Dynamics (soft-loud)
    • The dynamic level of the music with listening volume disregarded.
  • Harmonic Complexity (simple-complex)
    • The complexity of the harmonic progression in the song.
  • Modality (minor-major)
    • The modality of the song on a continuous scale from minor to major.
  • Pitch (low-high)
    • The general pitch height of the music.
  • Rhythmic Clarity (flowing-firm)
    • The rhythmic accentuation of the music.
  • Rhythmic Complexity (simple-complex)
    • A companion to the rhythmic clarity which measures the complexity of the rhythmic patterns.
  • Speed (slow-fast)
    • The speed of the music, based on how fast the music feels rather than measured BPM.
  • Valence (negative-positive)
    • The emotion of the song, which ranges from negative to positive.

Reference list of the perceptual features

Some perceptual features may require musical knowledge to understand, these are listed as complex features.

Simple features

  • Activity (low-high)
    The activity of the music.
  • Valence (negative-positive)
    The emotion of the song, which ranges from negative to positive. A sad song would have a negative valence, and a happy song would have a positive valence.
  • Speed (slow-fast)
    The speed of the music, based on how fast the music feels.
  • Modality (minor-major)
    The modality of the song on a continuous scale from minor to major. In general (for western music), a song in minor will sound darker or softer than a song in major.
  • Articulation (legato-staccato)
    The duration of tones, measured from legato (longest) to staccato (shortest). See this youtube video for a quick explanation and example of staccato and legato.
  • Dynamics (soft-loud)
    The overall dynamic level of the music, disregarding the listening volume. Dynamics is related to how loud a musical piece feels.
  • Pitch (low-high)
    The general pitch height of the music. Pitch is what makes a sound "high" or "low". See this youtube video for a short example.
  • Dissonance (consonant-dissonant)
    The dissonance of the music. See this youtube video for a short demonstration of consonance vs dissonance.

Complex features

  • Harmonic Complexity (simple-complex)
    The complexity of the harmonic progression in the song. It intends to reflect things like the amount of chords changes or deviations from a key scale structure.
  • Rhythmic Clarity (flowing-firm)
    The amount of rhythmic accentuation of the music.
  • Rhythmic Complexity (simple-complex)
    A companion to the rhythmic clarity which measures the complexity of the rhythmic patterns.
